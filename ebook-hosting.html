Best Way to Host an Ebook with Course Creator 360

Unlock the potential of your ebook by seamlessly integrating it into your Course Creator 360 platform. This step-by-step guide will show you how to effectively host and deliver your ebook to engage your audience and grow your business.

Introduction

Offering an ebook is a powerful way to attract and engage your audience, serving as a valuable lead magnet or a product offering. With Course Creator 360 (CC360), you have all the tools at your disposal to host, deliver, and promote your ebook efficiently. In this tutorial, we'll walk you through the best practices for hosting an ebook using CC360.

Step 1: Upload Your Ebook to CC360's File Manager

The first step is to upload your ebook file to your CC360 account, making it accessible for delivery.

1. Access the File Manager

  • Log in to your CC360 dashboard.
  • Navigate to Sites from the main menu.
  • Select Media in the sidebar.
  • Click on Add File.

2. Upload Your Ebook

  • Click on Upload File.
  • Choose your ebook file (e.g., PDF format) from your device.
  • Wait for the upload to complete.

3. Copy the File Link

  • Locate your uploaded ebook in the media library.
  • Click on the three dots next to the file name.
  • Select Copy Link.
  • Save this link for later use in your email or thank-you page.

Step 2: Create an Opt-in Landing Page

Design a compelling landing page to collect visitor information before they receive your ebook.

1. Access the Funnel Builder

  • From your dashboard, go to Funnels & Websites.
  • Click on Funnels.
  • Select Create Funnel and name it (e.g., "Ebook Opt-in Funnel").

2. Design Your Landing Page

  • Within your new funnel, add a New Step and choose Opt-in Page.
  • Select a template that aligns with your branding or start from scratch.
  • Use the drag-and-drop editor to customize the page:
    • Headline: Clearly state the value of your ebook.
    • Images: Include a cover image or preview of your ebook.
    • Description: Highlight the benefits and what readers will learn.
    • Opt-in Form: Add fields for Name and Email.
    • Call-to-Action: Use a prominent button like "Download Now" or "Get the Free Ebook".

3. Set Up the Opt-in Form

  • Click on the form element.
  • Ensure the form collects at least the Name and Email.
  • Configure the form to Submit data to your contact list.

Step 3: Set Up an Automation to Deliver the Ebook

Automate the delivery process so users receive the ebook instantly after signing up.

1. Create a New Workflow

  • Navigate to Automation in the main menu.
  • Click on Create Workflow.
  • Choose Start from Scratch.

2. Configure the Trigger

  • Set the Trigger to Form Submitted.
  • Select the opt-in form from your landing page.

3. Add an Email Action

  • Click on the Plus (+) icon to add a new action.
  • Choose Send Email.

4. Compose the Delivery Email

  • Write a personalized email thanking the user.
  • Include the download link you copied earlier.
  • Example: "Click here to download your free ebook."

5. Activate the Automation

  • Review your workflow for accuracy.
  • Toggle the Publish switch to activate.

Step 4: Create a Thank-You Page

Provide immediate feedback to users after they submit the form.

1. Design the Thank-You Page

  • In your funnel, add a New Step and select Thank-You Page.
  • Customize the page with a thank-you message.
  • Optionally, include the ebook download link directly on this page.

2. Set Up Form Redirect

  • Return to your opt-in form on the landing page.
  • Under Settings, configure the Redirect URL to point to your thank-you page.

Step 5: Nurture Your Leads

Engage with your new contacts to build relationships and encourage future interactions.

1. Create a Nurture Campaign

  • In Automation, create a new workflow.
  • Use the same Trigger as before or based on a Tag Added.

2. Develop an Email Sequence

  • Add a series of Email actions spaced out with Wait periods (e.g., wait 2 days).
  • Provide valuable content related to your ebook's topic.
  • Include tips, resources, or exclusive offers.

3. Segment Your Contacts

  • Use Tags to categorize users who downloaded your ebook.
  • This allows for targeted marketing and personalized communication.

Conclusion

By following these steps, you've effectively hosted your ebook on Course Creator 360 and established a seamless system for delivering it to your audience. This not only enhances the user experience but also opens avenues for ongoing engagement and business growth.
